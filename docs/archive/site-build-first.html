EcoDrop

Gravity battery ΓÇö built in a garage, carried through three years of science fairs, now four storage architectures and an elevator retrofit.
Amaan S. Khan ┬╖ Aiman Ullah ┬╖ Kavin Paturu Muralikrishnan
Chantilly High School ┬╖ 2024 ΓÇô present
Abstract

This study evaluates the energy efficiency of four small-scale gravitational storageΓÇôregeneration concepts ΓÇö buoyant displacement, electromagnetic assist, dual-weight counterbalance, and variable counterweight ΓÇö using a MATLAB simulation with strict end-to-end energy bookkeeping. Each design is modeled under identical baseline conditions and assessed by generated electrical energy per drop, electrical energy required for lift, round-trip efficiency (Eout/Ein and Eout/(mgh)), peak power, and detailed loss attribution. The analysis ranks concepts by realistic round-trip efficiency and identifies dominant mechanical and electrical loss channels. It also evaluates when active control elements produce a net benefit after accounting for coil and actuation energy. Reproducible test protocols and parameter files are provided to guide prototype selection and future experimental validation.

The build ΓÇö what we actually made

Autumn 2024: barbell masses on polypropylene rope, through a pulley, onto a hoist drum, through a custom gearbox, into a BLDC motor run as a generator. ~9 ft wooden scaffold in a garage. The first spool was a discarded bicycle wheel. The first gearbox was Vex/Lego parts. Neither worked well enough ΓÇö so we kept rebuilding.

Engineering logbook

Every gearbox generation, spool redesign, motor swap, and failed mechanism is documented in the 50-page engineering logbook ΓÇö not a summary, the actual iteration record.

Measured end-to-end efficiency rises with mass ΓÇö 18.7 % at 10 kg to 28.7 % at 25 kg (Γëê45 W average electrical output). These trials are what calibrated the MATLAB models in Year 2. Raw data: prototype-2024-25/data/measured_results.csv.

Four systems we designed

Year 2: after the prototype worked, we asked which architecture makes small-scale gravity storage practical. Four candidates ΓÇö CAD in Onshape, modeled in MATLAB, explained in 3D demos. None of these exist as full physical machines yet; they're designs calibrated against the Year 1 measurements.

Variable Counterweight

regenerative ┬╖ elevator-native

Modular masses deploy and retract to match the counterweight to the instantaneous load, so the machine supplies only residual imbalance plus friction. Each module recovers its own descent energy into onboard supercapacitors. Derived from how real elevator counterweights are sized per building.

77.0 ┬▒ 0.5 % DE
rank 1/4 ┬╖ simulated

Dual Weight

regenerative ┬╖ machine-room topology

Two hoist drums on one shaft with counter-wound ropes: as one mass descends the other ascends, and the machine generates on both directions of travel. The commercial analogue is the regenerative elevator drive; this design regenerates whenever the car moves.

60.0 ┬▒ 1.2 % DE
rank 2/4 ┬╖ simulated

Buoyancy

storage ┬╖ hydraulic lift

A dual-layer flooding chamber lifts the mass by displacement ΓÇö no lifting motor ΓÇö then a controlled descent through the generator discharges. Three chamber iterations isolate the rope and wooden structure from water; in-path turbines add secondary recovery.

39.5 ┬▒ 1.8 % DE
rank 3/4 ┬╖ simulated

Halbach Linear Machine active

storage ┬╖ electromagnetic

Tubular linear machines with T-shaped quasi-Halbach magnetization (after Kermani et al. 2018) lift the mass with no mechanical chain at all ΓÇö contact-free, position-controlled. Lowest simulated efficiency; highest research interest, and the current design focus.

27.0 ┬▒ 2.1 % DE
rank 4/4 ┬╖ simulated

Math model

All four architectures share one physics core; each adds its own loss channel. Energy bookkeeping is end-to-end ΓÇö gravitational potential in, electrical energy out ΓÇö with losses resolved per drivetrain stage at each timestep.

$$E_g = m\,g\,\Delta h$$
(1)
$$\mathrm{DE} \;=\; \frac{\displaystyle\int_{0}^{t_d} V(t)\,I(t)\,\mathrm{d}t}{m\,g\,\Delta h}\times 100\,\%$$
(2)
$$J_{\mathrm{eff}}\,\dot{\omega} \;=\; \frac{T_{\mathrm{load}}}{N} \;-\; T_{\mathrm{fric}}(\omega)\;-\;K_t\,I,\qquad J_{\mathrm{eff}} = J_m + \sum_i \frac{J_i}{\eta_i N_i^{2}}$$
(3)
$$V = K_e\,\omega_m - I\,R_m,\qquad K_t = \frac{60}{2\pi K_v} \approx 0.0405\ \mathrm{N\,m/A}$$
(4)

Eq. (1) ΓÇö stored energy per cycle; at the 50 kg / 3 m baseline, Eg = 1471.5 J for every system. Eq. (2) ΓÇö discharge efficiency, the primary metric (same metric used to rate pumped hydro and Li-ion). Eq. (3) ΓÇö rotational dynamics integrated by ode45; inertias reflected through gear stages. Eq. (4) ΓÇö machine model of the SK3-5065-236KV (Kv = 236 rpm/V, Rm = 19 m╬⌐).

Simulation protocol

  • Common baseline ΓÇö identical mass, height, drum, gear ratio, and machine model across systems; only the architecture differs.
  • Calibration ΓÇö friction and efficiency parameters fitted to the ┬º1 prototype measurements.
  • Replication ΓÇö 10 runs per system with motor efficiency perturbed ┬▒2 % and friction ┬▒5 %; means ┬▒ SD reported.
  • Inference ΓÇö one-way ANOVA across systems on discharge efficiency and net energy per cycle.
  • Sensitivity ΓÇö all friction parameters swept ┬▒20 %; the ranking is unchanged.
